An obituary in yesterday's paper is all that remembers him who sang such soft, sweet songs of the changing seasons. He sang this all in a voice cut with cigarettes and whisky. How hot ashes and hard liquor burn away all those fruitless insincerities, leaving only the bittersweet truth of a life full of regret. Everything that was once and no longer is, everything that could have been and never will, all those moments slipped away, all those wasted words that never did what they were supposed to. Every hope and ideal crushed, every dying light stomped out to be left in the deafening silence wherein man's propensity for self-destruction is left to fester. But when there is nothing left to lose, when there is no more pain left to feel and no more hatred left to fuel, when the fire of catharsis has finally burned out, there is nothing left to do but listen.

He sang of spring, who grew a grassy field of daffodils in her image. These she plucked merrily, and though he winced at every broken stem, he couldn't help but smile when he saw her braided hair so beautifully gilded with those delicate golden petals. He watched her dream away the days under the shade of those chattering oaks. That sun who shone warmly so that she might take off her sweater and feel her skin against the grass. For her, the flowers all rose to watch.

He sang of summer whose sweet berries left red-blue stains on the corners of her lips. His nights were comfortably cool, and he parted the clouds to let the moon and the stars shine oh so brightly. Some noon days he could be too hot to bear, but it was alright, because for her, the age-old glaciers melted into cool streams and lush meadows.
