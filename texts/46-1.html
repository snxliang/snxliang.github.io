A young girl grew up in a small village at the foot of the mountains in the north. Her father, a woodcutter, cared for her tenderly, though quietly. At dawn, he set out into the mountains with his long-handled axe slung over one shoulder. At dusk, she heard his faint whistle growing nearer, faint echoes down the valley.

So still she hadn't noticed, an old man sat in the shadow of a red pine. Though her father warned against bandits and evil spirits, this man seemed neither.

She could not tell whether his eyes were open, nor that he acknowledged her presence. Only a faint smile.

"Hello?"

His eyes, clearly open now, glimmered as the moon in a lily pond.

"Hello."

"I'm sorry for disturbing you, is this your home?"

"I reside here, but it does not belong to me."

"Then whose is it?"

He thought for a while how to respond, but could not before she interrupted again:

"Are you a bandit who steal all my things?"

"No."

"Are you an evil spirit who eats small children?"

"No."

"Are you a woodcutter, like my father?"

"No, though I sometimes cut wood."

"Are you a farmer?"

"No, though I sometimes grow food."

"Are you a monk? Sometimes these strange men who look a bit like you come to our village and beg for rice."

"Ah... No," he laughed slightly, "though I wouldn't mind some rice."

He gestured to a small misshapen bowl which had only a few green pinecones in it.

"Well if you're not a woodcutter, a farmer, or a monk then what are you?"

He laughed again. "Are those all the types of men you know?"

"Um, yeah, I guess."

"Your village is awfully small my friend! But alas, I shouldn't tease you so much. The people in your village call me Red Pine, because I live under a red pine. I don't much like that name." He touched the bark of the tree and gestured to its thick roots and gnarled branches. "This red pine is red pine. I am not red pine, I sit under red pine, but I am not red pine."

"Oh, I remember now, I think I've heard of you before. They say you've lived here for nine thousand years and that you know the secret to living forever."

Person-sitting-under-red-pine brightly laughed, "Do you believe them?"

"Well, that's just what the grown-ups in the village told me."

"They say many things..."

"So if you're not a woodcutter, a farmer, a monk, or red pine, then what are you?"

"I'm sorry I'm not much good at talking. Perhaps tomorrow you'll help me practice."





These days, I don't write much anymore. I don't really know why, but I just stopped talking to myself in that way. And that's really what it is, this talking to myself. It's the same thing as writing to me. Like, I remember back then I'd used to walk around the streets of this strange and decaying city, and just edit these stories in my head, which were in a sense daydreams. It was like my mind's way of processing my memories and emotions, and for some reason the output of that was these vague strings of concepts forming a sort of mental storyboard, which I'd every now and then find the energy to sit down and actually put into writing that someone else could read. Except in translating the rather non-verbal emotions in my mind to the rather clunky machinery of the English language, I usually end up coming up with most of what I write on the spot and taking things in a completely new direction. It's kind of like how I imagine jazz muzicians improvising where they compose the head and tail of a piece in advance, but let the solos arise in the moment. I don't know, I'm a terrible musician. But anyways, it feels like writing isn't just an activity one does at will like say pottery or climbing, but rather it's something that one does involuntarily, like crying from sadness or heaving in exhaustion. Put more plainly, it's like a state of mind for me. One characterized actually by being really anti-social and depressed, like I find writing to be a product of just spending way too much time alone with my thoughts. And the truth is, that I've seemingly become more afraid of that now, and so often I have all these distractions around to keep me busy. I mean, I used to find being stuck alone with my thoughts just as unpleasant, and yet, there was something in me that kept putting myself in that situation and I don't really know why, or perhaps I've somehow forgotten. I mean, yeah, I get it. It seems kinda sad that I don't write as much anymore. I thought at least some of my stories were kinda cute. But sometimes I also would just rather do something else, you know what I mean? Like why would I sit there and think sadly by myself for hours when I can just go do something more interesting, like talk to people, and do things.
